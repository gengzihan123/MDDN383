Canterbury Biltong Store

The Quality Mark Standard ensures that the meat used is: Sourced from New Zealand animals that have not been treated with growth promotants or reacted to TB testing. Processed in licenced plants operating under a Quality Assurance programme covering animal welfare, food-safety and animal transport. Traceble back to the producing farm or where it originated from. Classified under an export classification system approved by the New Zealand Meat Board. Processed using a prescribed and appropriate electrical stimulation, chilling and ageing regime to ensure the tenderness standard has been met by the time the product is on sale. Licensed premises are audited by ASURE New Zealand.

Below is a brief summary of manufacturing process. Export-grade, QualityMark topside steak delivered All fat trimmed off before beef is sliced Beef cured and spiced without the use of preservatives Air dried in custom built dryers Sliced into tasty bite-size pieces Packaged in an inert atmosphere in a high oxygen-barrier pouch Delivered to market with a 12 month best-before date from date of pack. The end result is a protein and iron rich air-dried beef snack that is seen as a healthy food choice by our customers. More importantly it is an extremely tasty snack. Every 100g of Canterbury Biltong uses at least 235g of Quality Mark beef. The manufacturing and food safety systems adhere to the specifications of the New Zealand Food Standard Authority and the Woolworths Quality Assurance programme and are audited by ASURE New Zealand.
